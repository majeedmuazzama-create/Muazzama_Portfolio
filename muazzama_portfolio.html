How I Think About Outreach
The principles behind
every message I send.

Outreach that works isn't luck. It's a system. These are the core principles I apply across every campaign, sequence, and proposal.

01.

Impact Before Persuasion

Most outreach fails because it jumps straight to the pitch. You can't persuade someone who hasn't stopped to read. The first job of any message is to earn attention through specificity, pattern interruption, or genuine value.

02.

Value-First Always

The best follow-up I ever sent wasn't a reminder. It was a free insight. Reciprocity is real: when you give something genuinely useful before asking, people feel compelled to respond. Every touchpoint should deliver something, not just request something.

03.

Specificity Builds Trust

Generic emails get deleted. Specific emails get replies. Calling out a person's exact product, a specific problem you noticed on their listing, or a metric relevant to their niche signals that you actually did the work, and that you understand them.

04.

CTAs That Don't Create Friction

A weak CTA kills a strong email. But an aggressive CTA kills it faster. The best CTAs ask for something small and easy: a reply, a 15-minute call, a yes/no. The goal of outreach is to start a conversation, not close a deal in one email.

05.

Patience With Purpose

The Upwork deal I closed was 10 days in and most people would have given up. Persistence matters, but only when each follow-up adds value. Sending the same message twice is annoying. Sending a new insight is a reason to reply.

06.

The Close Is a Question

I learned from studying closing frameworks that the best closes never give a yes/no option. They ask which option. "Which package works for you?" forces a decision. Applied to outreach: "Would Thursday or Friday work for a quick call?" is always stronger than "Let me know if you're interested."
